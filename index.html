Type 2 Diabetes: Risk and Prevention

Description

Type 2 Diabetes is a chronic disease that affects how the body uses glucose. The body becomes resistant to insulin or does not produce enough, causing high blood sugar.

Type of Disease

Noninfectious — cannot spread between people.

Effects on the Body

  • Heart disease
  • Kidney failure
  • Vision loss
  • Nerve damage
  • Slow healing wounds

Risk Factors

  • Poor diet
  • Lack of exercise
  • Obesity
  • Family history

Determinants of Health

  • Access to healthcare
  • Income and education
  • Access to healthy foods
  • Safe environments for exercise

Populations at Risk

  • Adults over 40
  • Hispanic and African American populations
  • Sedentary lifestyle

Prevention Strategies

Primary: Healthy lifestyle (diet, exercise)

Secondary: Screenings and early detection

CDC Data

Over 37 million Americans have diabetes and 1 in 5 do not know they have it.
